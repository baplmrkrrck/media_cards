Back Muscles

Trapezius

The Trapezius muscle is made up of two different heads, namely, the upper and lower Trapezius. The job of this particular muscle is to manipulate the Scapula (shoulder blade) and provide upper body stability.

Latissimus Dorsi

The largest visible muscle in the back, the Latissimus Dorsi is responsible for pulling the shoulder into place. It also provides stability to the Thoracic (middle back) and the Lumbar (lower back) spine.

Rhomboid

The Rhomboid is a fairly small muscle, it mostly lies just beneath the Trapezius and therefore is very hard to see. The entire job of the muscle is to retract the Scapula (shoulder blade), pulling it closer to the spine.

Teres Major

The Teres Major is part of a set with the Teres Minor and lies just above the Latissimus Dorsi. The job of this muscle is to work with the Posterior Deltoid and pull the arm behind the back.

Posterior Deltoid

The Posterior Deltoid is actually a single muscle head of the shoulder muscle known as the Deltoid. Although most commonly considered a shoulder muscle it plays a vitial role in how the back operates. Working in concert with the Teres Major to pull the arm to a posterior position.

Infraspinatus

The Infraspinatus is one of four muscles that make up the Rotator Cuff. These muscles are responsible for external and internal rotation of the arm. The Infraspinatus itself, works with the Teres Minor and Superspinatus to externally rotate while the Subscapularis just internally rotates.

Leg Muscles

Biceps Femoris

The Biceps Femoris is a part of the group of muscles known as the Hamstrings. The Hamstrings and the Biceps Femoris are agonist muscles to the Quads and their main role is to pull the foot closer to the butt.

Gluteus

The gluteus goes by many names and is a powerful structure in the body. It's main job is to contract and push the hips forward.

Adductors

The Adductors run from the hip complex to the Femur. Their job is to contract to pull the legs closer to the center line of the body.

Vastus Medialis

The Vastus Medialis is a part of a group of muscles largely known as the Quads. Working together with these other muscles the Vastus Medialis creates knee extension pulling it straight.

Psoas Major

The Psoas Major, as known as the hip flexor, is responsible for creating flexion in the hip joint. This means that when the muscle contracts it pulls the legs closer to the stomach.

Rectus Femoris

The Rectus Femoris is an active member in both the hip flexors and the Quads. While it is by far the weakest of both groups it plays an important roll in both knee extension and hip flexion.
